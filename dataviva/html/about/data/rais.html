{% trans %}Description{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}RAIS is an Administrative Registry created annually for the purpose of supplying the control, statistics, and information needs of governmental entities in the social area. It is an indispensable instrument for complying with legal norms, and is also of fundamental importance in the monitoring and description of the formal labor market. It describes the situation of companies on December 31st of the reference year and is composed of information declared annually by the companies themselves in relation to their employees, economic activity, and stock, among other characteristics. It currently covers around 97% of the Brazilian formal market.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Main Variables{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Jobs on December 31st, and those hired and released according to gender, age bracket, level of education, length of service and income, disaggregated in occupational, geographical and sector level. It contains information on the number of jobs by size of establishment, wage bill and employee nationality.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Data Availability{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Data is generally gathered between January and March of the year following the research reference year. And data release takes place approximately eight months after the beginning of collection.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Level of Disclosure{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}According to geographical selections: for Brazil, Major Regions, Federation Units, Metropolitan and Municipal Regions; by sectors: sector, subsector, according to IBGE 80 classification and at the level of Section, Division, Group and Class as per CNAE 1.0 and CNAE 2.0; and occupational: Large Occupational Group, Main Subgroup, Occupational Subgroup, Occupational Family and Occupation.{% endtrans %}
